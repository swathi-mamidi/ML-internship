About Communicable Diseases

Communicable diseases are illnesses caused by infectious agents and can be spread from person to person. Understanding these diseases is crucial for global health and public safety.

Two significant communicable diseases that we focus on are COVID-19 and tuberculosis.

Impact on Global Health

COVID-19, caused by the novel coronavirus, has rapidly spread worldwide, leading to a global pandemic. It has affected millions of people, overwhelmed healthcare systems, and disrupted economies.

Tuberculosis, on the other hand, has been a persistent global health issue, particularly in developing countries. It is responsible for significant morbidity and mortality, affecting millions of individuals each year.
