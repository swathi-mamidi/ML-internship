About Outbreaks

An outbreak refers to the sudden occurrence of cases of a particular disease or health condition in a community or population. These outbreaks can range from localized incidents to widespread epidemics.

Stay informed about the latest outbreak statistics of all countries to keep track of the total number of cases and other important data.

Outbreak management involves surveillance, rapid response, treatment, and prevention efforts. Communication and public awareness campaigns play a vital role in educating the community about the outbreak, its symptoms, and preventive measures.

How COVID-19 Spreads

Coughing Or Sneezing

Coronavirus can spread if an infected person coughs or sneezes

Close Personal Contact

Direct skin-to-skin contact increases likelihood of infection.

Touching Infected Surfaces

Unsanitized or infected surfaces can increase the likelihood of infection

Symptoms of COVID-19

Common symptoms of COVID-19 include

  • Fever
  • cough
  • shortness of breath
  • sore throat
  • diarrhea
  • body aches
However, it's important to note that some individuals may be asymptomatic or experience mild symptoms.
